What are the Symptoms of COVID-19?

Covid-19 shares many of its most common symptoms with other viruses with symptoms ranging from mild to severe.

Here are common symptoms to watch out for:

Fever/Chills

Cough

Difficulty Breathing

Symptoms may develop anywhere from

2-14 days after exposure to the virus

When should you seek Emergency Medical Attention?

Older adults and people who have severe underlying medical conditions like heart of lung disease or diabetes are at higher risk for developing more serious complications from COVID-19 illness.

If anyone is showing these symptoms, please seek emergecy medical care immediately:

Trouble Breathing

Persistent Pain in Chest

New Confusion

Inability to wake or stay awake

Bluish Lips or Face

This is not a full list of symptoms. If any other symptoms are severe or concerning to you, please contact your medical provider.
Contact Emergency Services

Think You Need to Get Tested?

Closely monitor your symptoms. If your symptoms worsen or become severe, contact your doctor or seek emergecy care. If your symptoms remain mild but concerning, consider getting tested at a nearby testing center.
